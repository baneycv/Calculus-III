    Let f be a scalar field and 𝐅 a vector field in ℝ3 (3-dimensions). Determine if the following quantities are scalar fields, vector fields, or meaningless. If the quantity is not meaningless, simplify the expression.

    1. (a)

      ∇⋅(∇×𝐅).
      Solution: We interpret this as the divergence of the curl of a vector field. Let

      𝐅=(F1⁢(x,y,z),F2⁢(x,y,z),F3⁢(x,y,z)).

      Then the curl of 𝐅 is:

      ∇×𝐅=(∂F3∂y−∂F2∂z,∂F1∂z−∂F3∂x,∂F2∂x−∂F1∂y).

      Now take the divergence of this vector:

      ∇⋅(∇×𝐅) =∂∂x⁢(∂F3∂y−∂F2∂z)+∂∂y⁢(∂F1∂z−∂F3∂x)+∂∂z⁢(∂F2∂x−∂F1∂y)
      =∂2F3∂x⁢∂y−∂2F2∂x⁢∂z+∂2F1∂y⁢∂z−∂2F3∂y⁢∂x+∂2F2∂z⁢∂x−∂2F1∂z⁢∂y.

      Now group and cancel:

      (∂2F3∂x⁢∂y−∂2F3∂y⁢∂x)+(∂2F1∂y⁢∂z−∂2F1∂z⁢∂y)+(∂2F2∂z⁢∂x−∂2F2∂x⁢∂z)=0

      by equality of mixed partial derivatives assuming sufficient smoothness.

      Conclusion: This is a scalar field and always zero.

    2. (b)

      ∇(div⁡f)
      Solution: Here, f is a scalar field. The divergence operator is only defined on vector fields. So:

      div⁡f=∇⋅fis undefined.

      Since the inner expression is meaningless, so is the whole expression.

      Conclusion: This expression is meaningless.

    3. (c)

      ∇⋅∇f.
      Solution: Here, the gradient of the scalar field f, ∇f is a vector :

      ∇f=(∂f∂x,∂f∂y,∂f∂z).

      Now take the divergence of this vector:

      ∇⋅∇f = ∂∂x⁢(∂f∂x)+∂∂y⁢(∂f∂y)+∂∂z⁢(∂f∂z)
      = ∂2f∂x2+∂2f∂y2+∂2f∂z2
      = ∇2f

      This is called the Laplacian of f, and it produces a scalar field.

      Conclusion: This is a scalar field equal to the Laplacian of f.
