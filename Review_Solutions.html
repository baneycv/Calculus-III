    Evaluate the following limit: lim(x,y)→(3,3)x2+y2−9x+y−6.
    Solution: First, observe that direct substitution of x=3 and y=3 into the expression yields

    Numerator: x2+y2−9=32+32−9=9+9−9=9,
    Denominator: x+y−6=3+3−6=0.

    Thus, direct substitution results in a form 90, indicating that the limit might not exist or could be infinite.

    Let us examine the behavior of the limit along different paths approaching (3,3).

    Substituting x=3 into the expression:

    limy→332+y2−93+y−6 =limy→39+y2−9y−3
    =limy→3y2y−3.

    Near y=3, y2 is approximately 9. Thus, near y=3,

    y2y−3≈9y−3.

    As y→3+, y−3 is positive and small, so the fraction approaches +∞. As y→3−, y−3 is negative and small, so the fraction approaches −∞.

    Substituting y=3 into the expression:

    limx→3x2+32−9x+3−6 =limx→3x2+9−9x−3
    =limx→3x2x−3.

    Similarly, near x=3, x2 is approximately 9, thus

    x2x−3≈9x−3.

    As x→3+, x−3 is positive and small, so the fraction approaches +∞. As x→3−, x−3 is negative and small, so the fraction approaches −∞.

    Since the limit approaches +∞ from some paths and −∞ from others, the limit does not exist.

    Let f⁢(x,y)=x2⁢y+4⁢x⁢y−12⁢y2. Find and classify all critical points for the function.
    Solution: We first find the gradient of f, and determine the values of (x,y) that make it equal to the zero vector. Hence, we have that,

    ∇f=(2⁢x⁢y+4⁢y,x2+4⁢x−24⁢y)

    Setting the x-component of ∇f equal to 0,

    2⁢x⁢y+4⁢y=0

    we solve.

    We find that s that y=0 and x=−2. We now search for the x or y corresponding to y=0 or x=−2, respectively, such that fy=0. Letting

    x2+4⁢x−24⁢y=0,

    we let y=0, and solve for x, which gives us x=−4 and x=0. Thus, (0,0), and (−4,0) are critical points. Next, let x=−2 and solve for y.

    (−2)2+4⁢(−2)−24⁢y = 0
    −6−24⁢y = 0
    y = −16

    Hence, we have that (−2,−16) is a critical point as well.

    Next, in order to classify the critical points, we look at the Hessian matrix, and determine for which critical points t is positive definite (i.e. the determinant ,D>0) If it is not positive definite then all critical points would be saddle points. Otherwise, we test fx⁢x to see if it is positive or definite. Hence, finding the determinant of the Hessian, D, we see that

    D = det⁡(fx⁢xfx⁢yfy⁢xfy⁢y)
    = fx⁢x⁢fy⁢y−fx⁢y2

Lastly, at (−2,−1/6), we have that D=8>0. Hence, the Hessian matrix is positive definite, and fx⁢x<0 which means we have a relative maximum value at (−2,−16).
